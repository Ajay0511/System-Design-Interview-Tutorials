1. What is Scalability?

Concept:
Scalability is a system’s ability to handle increased load (users, requests, data) without performance degradation.

Key idea:
A scalable system grows smoothly, not by breaking.
It’s about capacity planning, not just speed.

Types:
Vertical scaling – add more CPU/RAM
Horizontal scaling – add more machines (preferred)

Follow-up interview thought:
Why horizontal scaling is harder → requires stateless services, distributed data, coordination.
